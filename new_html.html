World Energy Consumption

Energy plays a big part in daily life of humanity. In order to strive for a more sustainable future where everyone has equal access to clean and safe energy, we first have to understand the problems and issues with current energy consumption. This visualisation aims to provide a better understanding of the current energy consumption of the world.

It visualizes the total electricity consumption around the world. In this visualization, there is color hue used for the color choice. When the country consumes less electricity, then the color will be in light green. Likewise, the higher the electricity consumption, the darker the color it has such as from yellow to dark red. In this idiom, there is high electricity consumption in United States, Canada and Australia. Let's explore why these country have higher electricity consumption than other countries.


Global Electricity Consumption per Country

Let's find out how electricity consumption can affects the GDP per capita as well as the population by country. This scatterplot visualise the relationship between total electricity consumption and GDP per capita. The higher the GDP per capita, the higher the total electricity consumption it takes. In this visualisation, United States is the first-highest electricity consumption that consumes 1,013 Terawtt electricity which is double to Russia. However,many countries in Africa are experiencing lacks of electricity consumption with low GDP per capita.

Country Rank on Coal Electricity Consumption per years

This bump chart visualizes the country ranking on coal electricity consumption on years. The ranking is only obtained from the top 8 country. In this chart, developed countries like United States, China, India remains the top 3 that consume a lot of electricity created by coal in 20th century. On the other hand, there is fluctuation on the ranking for countries Australia and Russia over the years. However, there is a rise on the rank of South Africa. In 2016, South Korea has the sixth-largest coal electricity consumption in the world compare to 2001 which is only in rank 11.
