World Energy Consumption

Energy plays a big part in daily life of humanity. In order to strive for a more sustainable future where everyone has equal access to clean and safe energy, we first have to understand the problems and issues with current energy consumption. This visualisation aims to provide a better understanding of the current energy consumption of the world.

Global Electricity Consumption per Country

This section explores the relationship between a countries wealth and its energy consumption. Countries with higher GDP such as the United States consume more electicity than lower GDP countries such as India with a ratio of 12. To put in perspective, the average Indian consumes the same amount of electicity in a month than the average American does in a Year.

Additionally, the poorest contries in the world consumed so little energy that they were not included in the dataset. As such, providing affordable energy is a key factor these countries

Primary Energy Change over the years

Burning traditional biomas was the dominant source of energy in the world until the mid-19th century. Only in recent time around 1980s did Wind and other renewable energy sources added.

Energy source distribution

Oil is the global primary energy source in the world. However, other renewable energy sources such as Hydro is growing rapidly.


Towards a low-carbon future

Countries such as Brazil and China are leading the way in decarbonizing our energy system. Poorer countries however, are still heavily dependent on Oil until they are able to grow their economies and switch to cleaner energy sources.

Each country has a role to play in providing cheap clean energy to the world. Reducing the fossil fuels burend and convering to renewable energy sources is the first step towards a low-carbon future.
